Hey — I've been doing some research on an idea and I think you might be the right person to help me figure it out. You mentioned the Sweden Solar System once — the one that stretches across the whole country. That got me thinking.

What if we built something like that right here in Vaughan? Not permanent sculptures or anything expensive — just a set of markers placed at the mathematically correct scaled distances, so you and your friends could actually experience how far apart the planets really are.

I've done some initial research and rough planning, but this is your project as much as mine. I need your input on the details — which objects to include, where the markers should go, how to make them. Scroll through this brief and let me know what you think. There's a feedback form at the bottom where you can add anything you want.

01 — The concept

What are we actually building?

A scale model of the solar system laid out across real Vaughan geography — parks, trails, and landmarks — that you can visit by bike or car.

Inspiration: The Sweden Solar System is the world's largest scale model of the solar system. The Sun is represented by a giant arena in Stockholm, and the planets are spread across the country — Neptune is 300 km away. We're doing a smaller version, sized to fit Vaughan.

The mind-blowing part: At this scale, Earth is only 0.043 mm across — invisible to the naked eye. The Sun is a 4.6 mm bead. Yet Earth is still 500 metres away from that bead. The empty space is the lesson.

03 — The route

Where should the planets go?

This is one of the most important parts of the project — and one that needs your input. Each planet has a fixed distance from the Sun, but it can be placed in any direction, like a hand on a clock. The goal is to find spots that are easy to reach, publicly accessible, and safe — parks, schoolyards, community centres, trail entrances.

04 — Budget

What this actually costs

Almost everything on this list can be done for free or nearly free. Here's a realistic breakdown.

  • Painted rocks or sticks
    Found outside, painted with leftover craft paint
    Free
  • Printed planet cards
    Print at school, library, or community centre
    Free
  • Laminating (optional)
    School or library often has a laminator
    Free
  • Permanent markers
    For writing on rocks / cards
    ~$2
  • Craft paint (small set)
    Dollar store — one set covers everything
    ~$3–5
  • The Sun bead (4.6mm gold)
    Craft store or dollar store bead bag
    ~$1–2
  • Chalk (for temporary markers)
    For testing locations before making permanent ones
    ~$2
Realistic total budget $0 – $12
Note on the permanent vs. temporary approach: We could do a fully temporary version first using chalk and printed cards, just to walk or bike the route and feel the distances. Then make permanent markers later for any locations where that makes sense (i.e. parks that would allow it). What do you think?
05 — Open questions

Things I need your help with

These are the decisions I don't want to make without you. Think about them and share your thoughts in the form below.

  • 1
    Do the suggested locations make sense to you? You know these parks and streets better than I do. Are there better spots for any of the markers?
  • 2
    What other objects should we add? There are thousands of things in the solar system we haven't included — moons, asteroids, spacecraft, dwarf planets. What do you think is missing?
  • 3
    How should we make the markers? Painted rocks? Wooden stakes with cards? Chalk for temporary? Something else entirely?
  • 4
    Should we do a test run first? We could walk or bike just the inner planets first (Sun to Mars — under 1 km!) to see how it feels before planning the full route.
  • 5
    What should the cards say? Each marker could include the planet name, its real distance, a size comparison fact, and maybe a fun fact. What info would you want on yours?
  • 6
    Is the scale right? At 1:300,000,000, Neptune is about 15 km away. We could go smaller (fits everything in a few km — easy bike ride) or bigger (outer planets are further — more impressive). What feels right?
